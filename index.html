Understanding Shoulder Subluxation

A shoulder subluxation occurs when the upper arm bone partially dislocates from the shoulder socket. This can lead to pain, swelling, and limited mobility. The good news is that many athletes recover fully from such injuries with proper treatment and rehabilitation.

What is the recovery timeline for a shoulder subluxation?

Recovering from a shoulder subluxation varies significantly based on severity and treatment options chosen. Generally speaking:

Typical Recovery Process

  1. Initial Rest: The first step is to rest the shoulder to prevent further damage.
  1. Physical Therapy: Engaging in physical therapy can help restore strength and range of motion.
  1. Gradual Return: Depending on pain levels and mobility improvements, athletes often begin light activity before returning fully.

Factors Influencing Recovery Time

Several factors can influence how quickly Ohtani recovers:

  • Severity of Injury: A more severe subluxation may require longer recovery.
  • Age and Fitness Level: Younger athletes often recover more quickly than older ones.
  • Treatment Approach: Whether he opts for conservative treatment or surgical intervention will also impact recovery time.
